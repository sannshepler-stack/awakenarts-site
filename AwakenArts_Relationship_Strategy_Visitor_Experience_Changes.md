# AwakenArts Relationship Strategy — Visitor Experience Changes

**Status: proposed, 2026-06-29.** This translates the Relationship Strategy's five stages into what a visitor would actually encounter differently — content, moments, invitations — not code or architecture. Like the Relationship Strategy itself, this is Publishing Track material: a first articulation for Susan to react to, not a build order. Where a change corresponds to something the Implementation Log's expansion sequence or the Implementation Specification already names, that connection is called out explicitly rather than left implicit — this document isn't proposing a new sequence, only filling in what should actually happen at each step the sequence already plans for.

---

## 1. After one visit — relationship: an unclaimed first encounter

**Today.** The homepage already does most of this correctly: no early email gate, no pressure, two open invitations (Encounters, Collection), quality demonstrated rather than asserted. The Relationship Strategy's guidance here was mostly *protective* — don't add anything that would turn this into a captured lead.

**The changes.**

- **Leave the restraint alone.** No new CTA, gate, or ask belongs at this stage. The one real risk to this relationship is over-eagerness, not under-asking.
- **One earlier, quieter signal of difference.** A single line — not the full Matthew 13:34 passage, just a hint of it — placed near the hero or the Collection section, so a first-time visitor brushes against "why this is different" before they decide whether to keep going, not only after. This stays a hint, not an argument; the full argument still belongs where it is now, near the close of the homepage.
- **One sentence naming what a Figure Edition is.** Right now a first-time visitor has to infer the term from repetition. A single plain sentence — what one of these actually is, in the Collection section or wherever a visitor first meets the term — removes that ambiguity without adding any explanatory weight the page wasn't already carrying.

---

## 2. After reading one Figure Edition — relationship: a reader, proven, wanting more

**Today.** This is the largest gap. An Edition page ends the instant the PDF link is opened — no closing moment, no path forward except back to the grid, and the page doesn't even carry the site's standard navigation band, so a visitor who arrived there directly is genuinely stuck.

**The changes.**

- **Give the Edition page a closing moment.** After someone has read the work, the page should offer something — not a request, an invitation to continue the relationship they're already in: being told when the next Figure Edition exists. This is the single highest-impact change identified across both the Presentation Evaluation and the Relationship Strategy, and it corresponds directly to the Acquire step already planned in the Implementation Specification (Phase 6) and the Log's expansion sequence — this document isn't asking for something new, it's naming why that already-planned step matters from the relationship side.
- **Restore the standard navigation.** Whatever else changes, a reader who finishes an Edition shouldn't be limited to "back to the Collection." The same Wayfinding Band every other page carries should carry through here too, so the relationship has somewhere to go besides backward.
- **A forward path, not just a backward one.** Alongside "back to the Collection," a quiet invitation to a specific next Edition — not a list, one suggestion — keeps the reading relationship moving in the direction it was already going, rather than returning the visitor to a decision they'd already made.

---

## 3. After purchasing one Collection — relationship: a steward, trusted with more

**Today.** Nothing exists yet — no purchase mechanism, and no page frames the Collection as a single ownable thing distinct from the always-free browsing grid. This stage is entirely prospective.

**The changes, once this stage exists.**

- **A Collection needs its own page, separate from the grid.** Someone deciding whether to own the whole body of work is making a different decision than someone browsing individual Editions — the visitor experience should treat it as one, with its own framing of what's included and why it's worth holding onto as a whole.
- **The relationship shouldn't end at "thank you."** What an owner sees after purchasing should include a sense of continuity — that more is coming, in the same unhurried voice as everything else — and access proportionate to having bought the whole, not just one piece: the deeper "how it's used" material (Reflection, Discussion, Teaching, Workshops, Journaling) the Collection page already names as categories but doesn't yet deliver as actual content for anyone, owner or not.
- **Sequencing note.** This corresponds to "Commerce activation" and "Collections" in the Log's already-recorded expansion sequence (items 3 and 4) — both come after the Reader is proven and generalized, which is why none of this is a visitor-facing change today.

---

## 4. After attending a workshop — relationship: a practitioner, social, multiplying

**Today.** Workshop materials exist but are deliberately excluded from navigation, the Wayfinding Band, and the Footer — reachable only by a direct link nobody on the visible site ever hands a visitor. This is the one stage where something real has been built and a visitor still cannot find their way to it at all.

**The changes.**

- **Give Workshops one quiet, discoverable entry point.** Not a prominent nav item competing with Encounters and Collection — something more like how Foundation was added (footer-level, or a single link from the Collection page's existing "Workshops" tag, which currently sits inert as a label rather than a link). The goal is findable, not promoted.
- **Carry the relationship past the workshop itself.** Someone who has practiced the work in a group has a different relationship than someone who read it alone — what they encounter afterward should acknowledge that: a way to bring AwakenArts to their own group, or a path toward facilitating it themselves, rather than reverting to the same generic "read another Edition" invitation everyone else gets.
- **Sequencing note.** Corresponds to "Workshop platform," item 5 in the Log's expansion sequence — later than Collections, which is consistent with this stage representing a deeper relationship than ownership alone.

---

## 5. After a retreat — relationship: a formed companion, in community

**Today.** No page, no mention, anywhere on the live site.

**The changes, once this stage exists.**

- **Minimal asks, by design.** Of all five stages, this is the one where the visitor experience should do the least — someone who has attended a retreat has already given AwakenArts more trust and time than any other relationship in this sequence. What follows should mostly just stay out of the way: simple continuity (when the next one is), and a way to stay in touch with the people they shared it with, more than a way to stay in touch with AwakenArts itself.
- **Community over funnel.** If anything is built here, it should look less like a marketing follow-up sequence and more like a quiet, ongoing space — closer to an alumni gathering than a customer relationship.
- **Sequencing note.** Corresponds to "Retreat platform," item 6 in the Log's expansion sequence — the furthest stage out, appropriately, since it represents the deepest relationship in the strategy.

---

## What doesn't change

Across all five translations, two things should stay constant, because they're what the Relationship Strategy named as the part of AwakenArts that shouldn't flex regardless of how much trust or money has changed hands: every invitation should read as continuation of a relationship already underway, never as a new ask bolted on, and nothing recommended above should require AwakenArts to sound louder, faster, or more conventional than it already does to work. Where a recommendation above would have failed that test, it isn't here.
