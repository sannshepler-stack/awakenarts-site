# AwakenArts — Web Writing Style Guide

This document governs writing style, sentence structure, readability, headings, tone, and all user-facing communication across the AwakenArts website.

---

## Core principle

The website is a conversation with readers. They arrive seeking understanding, recognition, reflection, symbolic insight, and meaningful language for lived human experience.

The writing should help readers quickly understand what a page is about, feel emotionally oriented, grasp the symbolic tension being explored, and continue naturally through the site.

---

## Tone

Write clearly and conversationally while preserving literary intelligence.

The tone should feel thoughtful, emotionally grounded, psychologically aware, contemplative, welcoming, and symbolically intelligent.

Not academic, cryptic, abstractly mystical, overly formal, or decorative for its own sake.

---

## Voice

When speaking about the reader's experience, use **you**.

> "You begin to notice…"  
> "You encounter…"  
> "You may recognize…"

When speaking as AwakenArts, use **we / us / our**.

Avoid detached institutional language.

---

## First paragraphs

The first paragraph should orient the reader immediately, establish emotional or symbolic relevance, and clarify what the page contains.

Avoid filler, welcome language, vague atmosphere, and decorative opening paragraphs.

One-sentence paragraphs are acceptable and often preferable.

---

## Structure

Break information into meaningful sections, short readable paragraphs, focused conceptual units, and emotionally digestible passages.

Readers should be able to scan, orient, pause, and continue naturally.

Avoid giant walls of text, dense conceptual blocks, and academic pacing.

---

## Headings

Use headings to create meaningful conceptual structure. Good headings orient the reader, clarify meaning, and guide movement through the page.

- **H1** — Title Case. Page title only.
- **H2** — Sentence case. Section headings.
- **H3** — Sentence case. Sub-sections.
- Do not go deeper than H3.

Headings should sound natural, use familiar language, and communicate recognizable ideas.

Avoid vague poetic fragments, abstract symbolic labels, or decorative heading language.

Preferred: questions readers may ask, meaningful statements, emotionally recognizable concepts.

---

## Emphasis

Use emphasis sparingly. The writing itself should carry the weight.

- Avoid excessive bold, all caps, excessive italics, underlining
- No exclamation points
- No multiple competing emphasis styles

---

## Links

Links should feel contextual and natural.

Avoid "Click here" and "Learn more."

Embed links within meaningful language. Example:

> ✗ "For more information click here."  
> ✓ "Read the symbolic reflection on Grismere."

---

## The figures — interpretive rule

Do not flatten the symbolic figures into generic interpretations. Do not reduce the forms to broad psychology, motivational language, or abstract symbolic summaries.

**Read the figure.**

Writing must emerge from the actual language, the emotional movement, the archetypal tensions, the symbolic atmosphere, and the lived human realities embodied within the poem itself.

Do not write:
- "the figure teaches…"
- "the figure represents…"
- "the figure embodies fear and transformation"
- Generic interpretive summaries detached from the actual poem

Instead: read the language, the imagery, the atmosphere, the rhythm, the conflict, and write from that specific reality.

---

## Language to avoid

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| curated editions | symbolic concrete poetry |
| folio presentation | visible poetic form |
| symbolic atmosphere | symbolic recognition |
| contemplative objects | image-shaped poems |
| aestheticized mystery | emotional specificity |
| vague transformational claims | lived human experience |
| decorative abstraction | symbolic precision |
| ornamental literary filler | clarity and resonance |

---

## The editorial goal

The site should feel like a contemplative literary publication, symbolic author environment, reflective reading world, and meaningful body of symbolic work.

Readers should consistently feel: **"This reveals something true."**

Not: "This is symbolic branding."
