# AwakenArts Platform Presentation Evaluation

**Status: evaluation, 2026-06-29, per Susan.** This is not architecture, not a specification, and not the Implementation Log — it sits alongside that three-tier hierarchy as a fourth, distinct kind of document: a diagnostic read of the site as it exists today, written for the visitor's experience, not the codebase. Per Susan's instruction, this evaluation does not address code or implementation; every observation below is grounded in what a visitor actually sees and can do on the live pages (Homepage, Collection, Encounters, the five Encounter pages, an Edition page, Foundation, About, Nav, Footer, and the Wayfinding Band), read in full before writing this.

---

## 1. First Impression

**What a first-time visitor should understand within 30 seconds.** That AwakenArts is a body of original symbolic art — image and poem built as one work — offered from a specific point of view, with more than one of these works to discover, and more than one way to begin.

**Does the homepage communicate that effectively?** Partly. In the first screen, a visitor reliably takes away three things: this is about "symbolic language," it works through image and poem, and there are two doors in (Encounters, Collection). What does not arrive in the first screen — and doesn't arrive until the very end of the page — is that AwakenArts is a publishing platform with a growing library of complete works, or why this is offered from a Christian literary tradition rather than being generic spiritual/poetic content. Both of those facts exist on the homepage. They're simply the last things said, not the first.

**What creates confidence.** The craft itself: a real painted figure (Queen Ann) given full visual weight, restrained and consistent typography, no stock imagery, no generic marketing language in the hero. A visitor's first impression of quality is earned honestly, by the work, not asserted by copy.

**What creates uncertainty.** The phrase "symbolic language" is used before it's ever demonstrated or defined — a visitor has to keep scrolling to find out what that actually means in practice. There's also no early signal of scope (how many works exist, whether this is a finished body of work or an ongoing one) or of what kind of site this is — portfolio, ministry, literary press, teaching practice. A visitor is asked to pick a door (Encounters or Collection) before they know what's behind either one.

**What should be strengthened or removed.** Strengthen: give the differentiation claim — that this work extends a literary tradition Scripture itself already uses — at least a brief, early appearance, rather than holding it exclusively for the homepage's last section. Reconsider: whether two equally-weighted invitations at the very top, before any orientation, ask too much of a visitor who hasn't yet been told what either choice leads to.

## 2. Brand Identity

**What AwakenArts is.** Reasonably clear, but assembled from three different pages rather than stated plainly in one place: the homepage implies it, the Collection page names the unit ("Figure Edition") and describes what one contains, and the Foundation page states the theological grounding outright. No single page says, in one place, all three things together.

**Who it serves.** Never stated anywhere. The Collection page's "How it's used" tags — Reflection, Discussion, Teaching, Workshops, Journaling — imply an audience (a personal reader, a small-group facilitator, an educator) without ever naming or addressing one directly. No page speaks to a visitor in the second person about who this is for.

**Why it is different.** Handled well where it appears — the homepage's Matthew 13:34 section and the Foundation page both make a genuine, specific claim (this work sits inside the same figurative tradition Scripture itself uses, not "art with a Bible verse attached"). The problem is placement: Foundation is deliberately excluded from primary navigation, and the homepage's version of this claim is the second-to-last thing on the page. A visitor who doesn't reach the bottom of the homepage or click into About may never encounter the one argument for why AwakenArts is different from any other symbolic/devotional art online.

**Why someone should continue exploring.** Implied by quality, never stated. No page tells a visitor what they'll find by continuing — how many Editions exist, that new ones are added over time, or what a complete reading experience actually contains.

**Ambiguity identified.** The term "Figure Edition" is used with full confidence across Collection, Poems, and the Edition pages themselves, but is never defined in plain language anywhere a first-time visitor is likely to land first. A visitor who enters through Encounters (which are explicitly not tied to any Figure or Edition) may never learn what a Figure Edition even is.

## 3. Website Presentation

**Visual hierarchy.** Strong and clearly intentional on every page — each one is built around a deliberate sequence (mood, then product, then example, then meaning) rather than a flat list of sections.

**Readability.** Good. Body copy is narrow, typography is restrained, nothing is dense or crowded.

**Navigation.** Mostly consistent — the primary Nav, the Wayfinding Band, and the Footer appear together on nearly every page, giving a visitor a constant, reliable way to keep exploring. The one significant exception: an individual Figure Edition page — the actual product page — replaces all of that with its own minimal footer offering only "Back to the Collection." A visitor who lands directly on an Edition (a shared link, a search result) has no way to discover Encounters, About, or anything else on the site except returning to the one page they came from.

**Pacing.** A genuine strength. Pages alternate cream and dark fields deliberately, and are paced to be read in order, like a book, rather than scanned like a typical marketing page.

**Imagery.** Original, consistent in tone, and used with restraint — no stock photography anywhere observed.

**Typography.** The most disciplined part of the site — a locked, documented system applied consistently across headings, eyebrows, quotations, and captions.

**Consistency.** High everywhere except the Edition page's footer, noted above.

**Emotional tone.** Quiet, literary, and contemplative throughout, matching the stated intent to "show more, explain less."

**Does it feel like a cohesive publishing platform?** Editorially and visually, yes — it reads like a small literary press's site, not a portfolio or a devotional blog. Functionally, not yet: there is no in-site reading experience for a Figure Edition (only a link out to a raw PDF), no moment on the actual product page that invites a visitor to do anything but leave or go back, and the one page most likely to convert a casual visitor into a returning one is currently the thinnest page on the site.

## 4. Visitor Journey

Tracing Homepage → Collection → Encounter → Figure Edition → Recognition → Acquire → Newsletter → Future Products as it exists today:

**Homepage → Collection.** Natural. The homepage's own Collection section already begins delivering on the promise before the click happens.

**Collection → Encounter.** Natural, and well-timed — Collection ends on its "Path of Recognition" graphic and moves directly into an "Enter Encounters" invitation while that imagery is still fresh.

**Encounter → Figure Edition.** This is where momentum breaks. Encounters are deliberately atmospheric and not tied to any figure — none of the five Encounter pages links to a specific Figure Edition. A visitor who arrives at Encounters from the homepage has no path forward into an Edition at all; they would have to leave Encounters and start over through Collection.

**Figure Edition.** Momentum slows sharply here, regardless of how a visitor arrives. After the designed richness of Collection and Encounters, the Edition page is the thinnest experience on the site: a title, one image, and a link that opens a large PDF in a new browser tab. The visitor leaves the website itself to encounter the actual work.

**Figure Edition → Recognition.** This step doesn't exist in this order. "A Path of Recognition" only appears as an explanatory graphic before a visitor reads an Edition (on Collection and Encounters) — never afterward, when it would actually help someone reflect on what they just read.

**Recognition → Acquire.** Doesn't exist. There is no Acquire moment anywhere on the site for any Edition.

**Acquire → Newsletter.** Also doesn't exist in this order. The one subscribe moment on the entire site — the free Encounter Journal — lives inside Encounters, unconnected to having just read any specific work.

**Newsletter → Future Products.** Nothing follows the one newsletter signup; there's no next invitation after it.

**Where momentum increases.** Steadily, from Homepage through Collection through Encounters — this is genuinely well-built and well-sequenced.

**Where it slows.** At the Figure Edition page — the website itself essentially ends there, handing the visitor off to a PDF.

**Where it stops.** Completely, after that point. There is no Recognition, Acquire, or Newsletter step that follows from having actually read a work — the site has a strong front half and currently no back half.

## 5. Figure Editions

**Do visitors understand what they are?** At a surface level — a title, a kicker, an image — but the term itself is never explained in plain language anywhere a first-time visitor is likely to encounter it first.

**Why they matter.** Gestured at, not stated, on the Collection page ("image, poem, and reflection together as a single published work") — but that line describes Figure Editions as a category, never a specific one.

**Why they are different.** Not addressed at the level of an individual Edition at all. Nothing on an Edition's own page argues why this particular work is worth a visitor's time over any other symbolic art or poetry available online.

**Why someone would want to own one.** This is the clearest gap. Every Figure Edition today is a free, unrestricted PDF. Nothing about the page creates a reason to want it as a possession rather than simply opening a link once — no sense of craft-as-object, no limited or numbered framing, no message connecting ownership to supporting future work. These are all things consistent with AwakenArts' literary identity that currently go unused.

## 6. Calls to Action

Every invitation currently presented to a visitor, in order encountered:

- Hero: "Enter the Encounters," "Explore the Collection"
- Homepage Collection section: "Browse the Figure Editions"
- Homepage Matthew 13:34 section: "Read the Foundation of AwakenArts"
- Collection page: "Enter Encounters"
- Encounters index: five links into individual Encounters; "Send Me the Journal" (the one email-gated download on the site)
- An Edition page: "View the Figure Edition (PDF)"; "Back to the Collection"
- About page: a quiet "Read the Foundation of AwakenArts" link

**Are they clear?** Yes — every one is plainly worded and well-placed within its own page.

**Are they appropriately timed?** Mostly, and the homepage/Collection sequencing in particular is well thought through. The real problem isn't timing of what exists — it's what's missing at the moments that matter most.

**Are any too early?** No instance observed asks for an email or any commitment before the work itself has made an impression — that restraint is a genuine strength, not a flaw.

**What's missing?** Three invitations that don't exist anywhere on the site: a subscribe invitation tied to a specific Edition or to Collection itself (placed at the moment interest peaks); any Acquire or purchase invitation, anywhere; and a "continue to another Edition" invitation from inside an Edition page, which currently offers only a single path backward, never forward into the next work.

**Which should become primary?** Once it exists, a subscribe invitation placed at the close of a Figure Edition — after the visitor has actually experienced the work — should outrank "Back to the Collection" as that page's primary next step. It asks at the moment of greatest interest instead of simply returning the visitor to where they started.

## 7. Trust

**What currently establishes credibility.** Susan's named, credentialed presence on the About page (MA in Counseling Psychology, Certified Transformative Language Artist, Journal Instructor certification); a stated theological foundation that is explicit about its own limits ("not doctrine, revelation, or spiritual authority"); consistently high-quality original imagery and typography throughout; a copyright notice on every page.

**Where the opportunity is.** Both the credentials and the theological foundation live outside the primary visitor journey — About is in the main navigation, but Foundation deliberately is not, and a visitor who never reaches the very bottom of the homepage or clicks into About may never see either one. There is no testimonial, no indication that anyone else — a workshop participant, a facilitator, a reader — has engaged with the work, and no visible sense of how long AwakenArts has existed or how much of it there is. None of these would require abandoning the site's restrained tone to add. Separately worth a look: the footer's second copyright line reads "© Susan Ann Shepler · Confidential" directly beside the public copyright notice — "Confidential" on an otherwise public, visitor-facing footer reads like an internal label that wasn't meant to be customer-facing, and could itself plant a small flicker of doubt in an otherwise trust-building element.

## 8. Marketing Readiness

**Edition launches.** A new Edition can be added to the Collection grid and the Poems gallery without redesigning either page — but nothing on the live site can announce a new Edition once it exists. There's no "Latest" callout, no dated content, no mechanism besides the one undifferentiated email list.

**Collection launches.** Nothing currently distinguishes "the Collection" as a single offering — there is no Collection-level page describing it as one purchasable thing (contents, format, price); it exists only as the always-free browsing grid.

**Workshops.** Built — curriculum, facilitator materials, intake and feedback forms all exist — but the /workshops page is deliberately left out of the primary Nav, Footer, and Wayfinding Band. A visitor cannot find Workshops by browsing the site at all.

**Retreats.** No page and no mention exists anywhere on the live site today.

**Facilitator resources.** A facilitator-orientation page exists but is equally undiscoverable by ordinary browsing.

**Future educational products.** No placeholder or reserved page architecture exists yet for this category.

**Structural limitations, in summary.** No announcement mechanism for new work; one undifferentiated email list rather than segmented interest; built Workshop and facilitator material that's invisible to anyone not handed a direct link; and no page that frames the Collection as a single product distinct from browsing individual Editions.

---

## 9. Customer Journey

**Subscribe.** Currently possible only by browsing all five Encounters cards to reach a free, Edition-unrelated download. Weak because it's disconnected from whatever actually earned the visitor's interest — there's no moment where subscribing follows directly from having just read a specific work.

**Return.** Nothing on the site currently invites a return visit — no indication of new or upcoming work, nothing dated, nothing time-bound. The homepage in particular reads as a complete, closed experience rather than an ongoing publication, which works against its own interest in being revisited.

**Purchase an Edition.** Never — no purchase mechanism exists anywhere on the site; every Edition is a free, unauthenticated PDF.

**Purchase a Collection.** Never, for the same reason, and additionally because the Collection isn't packaged anywhere as a single purchasable thing.

**Attend a workshop.** Never reachable through ordinary browsing — the page exists but nothing on the discoverable site points to it.

All five transitions share one root cause: AwakenArts currently has a complete, well-designed "discover and read" experience, and no "deepen the relationship" experience at all. Every moment where a visitor might naturally be invited to do something beyond continue reading simply isn't offered yet.

## 10. Strategic Recommendations

Ranked by impact. Each is chosen because it strengthens clarity, engagement, trust, or conversion without asking AwakenArts to behave like a typical marketing site — none of these recommend anything AwakenArts wouldn't already recognize as its own voice.

**1. Give each Figure Edition a closing moment, not just an opening one.** Right now a visitor's experience of an Edition ends the instant they open the PDF — there's no invitation waiting for them on the other side of having read it. A subscribe invitation specific to that Edition, framed as continuation ("hear when the next Figure Edition is published") rather than a generic newsletter ask, would capture interest at its peak instead of letting it dissipate. This is the highest-impact single change available, and it's consistent with what the Reader already being built (per the Implementation Specification) is designed to deliver — this recommendation reinforces work already underway rather than proposing a new direction.

**2. Stop the Edition page from being a dead end.** It's currently the one page on the site without the standard Wayfinding Band, meaning a visitor who arrives there directly has no way to discover anything else AwakenArts offers except going back to where they started. Giving it the same site-wide navigation every other page has — eventually replaced by the Reader's own closing section — fixes the single point on the site where momentum currently stops outright.

**3. Let the differentiation claim arrive earlier.** The Matthew 13:34 section is the strongest, most specific argument on the entire site for why AwakenArts isn't interchangeable with other symbolic or devotional work — and it's the second-to-last thing a visitor sees. A brief, quiet appearance of this idea nearer the top of the homepage would let a first-time visitor encounter the "why this matters" argument before, not only after, they've decided whether to keep exploring.

**4. Name the audience and define the unit of the work, once, early, and plainly.** Neither "who is this for" nor "what is a Figure Edition" is stated anywhere in plain language — both are only ever implied through repetition. A single clear sentence in the right place (the Collection page's opening, or the homepage's Collection section) would remove an ambiguity that currently asks every first-time visitor to infer the basics on their own.

**5. Make Workshops and facilitator resources findable.** Real, paid-for work already exists behind these pages, and none of it can be discovered by browsing the site. Adding a single, quiet entry point — consistent with how Foundation was added to the footer without crowding the primary navigation — would surface built value that's currently invisible to anyone without a direct link.

---

This evaluation describes the site as it exists today. It makes no claim about what should be built next beyond the five recommendations above — that remains a decision for the Implementation Specification's phases and Susan's own Publishing Track to take up in turn.
